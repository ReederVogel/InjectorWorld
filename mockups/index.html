FAQ

Common questions, plain answers.

How does injector.world verify providers? +
Every injector goes through three checks before they can list. License verification against the state medical board, credential review by our medical advisory board, and patient review moderation. We do not let injectors delete reviews. Read the full guide →
Is Botox safe? +
Botox has been FDA-approved since 1989 and is one of the most studied aesthetic treatments. When administered by a trained, licensed injector, the complication rate is low. Most side effects are mild and temporary. Read the full guide →
How much does Botox cost? +
National average is around $14 per unit. Most patients get 20 to 40 units across the forehead, glabella, and crow's feet, putting a typical visit between $280 and $560. NYC and LA run $16 to $18 per unit. Midwest and South are lower. Read the full guide →
What's the difference between an MD, NP, and RN injector? +
All can legally inject in most states, but the supervision and training requirements vary. Board-certified MDs in dermatology or plastic surgery have the most training. NPs and PAs often work under medical direction. RNs require physician oversight. Read the full guide →
How do I prepare for my first appointment? +
Avoid blood thinners (Aspirin, ibuprofen, fish oil, vitamin E) for 5 to 7 days before. Skip alcohol the night before. Come with clean skin, no makeup. Bring reference photos if you have specific goals. Ask about the brand they carry. Read the full guide →
Are reviews on injector.world real? +
Yes. Every review is checked for authenticity and tagged to a specific treatment. Reviewers must confirm their appointment was booked through us or provide a clinic receipt. Injectors cannot delete reviews, only respond to them. Read the full guide →
