# Homepage Plan — Plain English

This is what every section on the homepage does, why it's there, and what's inside it. Top to bottom.

---

## 1. Header (the bar at the top)

**What it is:** The thin bar that stays at the top of every page.

**Why it's there:** So visitors can move around the site no matter where they are.

**What's inside:**
- Logo on the left ("injector.world").
- 4 nav links in the middle: Treatments, Cities, Guides, How we verify.
- 2 small links on the right: "List your practice" (for injectors) and "Sign in" (for patients).
- On mobile, the 4 nav links collapse into a hamburger menu icon.

**What the visitor does here:** Clicks to navigate. The header follows them as they scroll so they can always jump somewhere else.

---

## 2. Hero (the big section at the top)

**What it is:** The first thing the visitor sees. Big serif headline, search bar in the middle.

**Why it's there:** To answer two questions in 5 seconds. *What is this site?* and *How do I use it?*

**What's inside:**
- Small green label at the top: "TRUSTED BY 87,000+ PATIENTS".
- Big headline: *Find a verified injector you can actually trust.*
- One-line subhead explaining what makes us different.
- A search bar with two fields: treatment (Botox, Lip Filler, etc.) and location (city or ZIP). Big "Search" button on the right.
- Small chips under the search: Botox, Lip Filler, Masseter, Tear Trough. Click them to skip typing.

**What the visitor does here:** Types their treatment and city. Hits Search. Goes to the city directory page.

---

## 3. Trust bar (the strip right below the hero)

**What it is:** A quiet strip with credentials and numbers.

**Why it's there:** Botox is a medical treatment. Visitors are nervous. We show trust signals before anything else.

**What's inside:**
- Label: "MEDICALLY REVIEWED BY".
- Four credential logos: ABMS, AAD, ASPS, ASDS (the main US medical bodies for derm and plastic surgery).
- Three big numbers separated by lines: *12,400 verified injectors · 87,000 patient reviews · 50 states*.

**What the visitor does here:** Nothing. Just reads it and feels more comfortable to keep scrolling.

---

## 4. Browse by Treatment

**What it is:** A grid of 12 treatment tiles. Botox, Dysport, Lip Filler, Cheek Filler, Jawline Filler, Tear Trough, Masseter, Kybella, Sculptra, PRP, Microneedling, Thread Lift.

**Why it's there:** Some visitors are not searching. They are browsing. They want to see what treatments exist before they pick one.

**What's inside each tile:**
- A small icon.
- The treatment name.
- Two links: "Read the guide" and "Find a provider".

**What the visitor does here:** Clicks a tile. Either goes to the educational guide (if they want to learn) or to the directory (if they want to book).

**Why this matters:** This is the visual moment where we show that content and directory are equal. Two clicks side by side on every tile.

---

## 5. Browse by City

**What it is:** A grid of 12 city tiles. NYC, LA, Miami, Chicago, Houston, Dallas, Atlanta, Phoenix, Seattle, Boston, DC, San Francisco.

**Why it's there:** Local intent is huge for this category. People search "Botox in Miami," not just "Botox."

**What's inside each tile:**
- A photo of the city in the background.
- The city name in big serif text.
- The number of verified injectors in that city. Example: "1,240 verified injectors".

**What the visitor does here:** Clicks their city. Lands on the city directory page.

**Below the grid:** A small link, "See all 200+ cities", which leads to a master list.

---

## 6. Featured Injectors

**What it is:** A row of 3 to 6 injector cards picked by our editors.

**Why it's there:** Patients trust other people's choices. Showing curated picks builds credibility and gives the visitor a starting point.

**What's inside each card:**
- A photo of the clinic at the top.
- A small "Editor's Pick" ribbon on the first card.
- The injector's headshot, name, and credentials (MD, DO, NP, etc.).
- A green check icon with "License verified" and their state license number.
- Star rating and review count.
- Three treatment tags (Botox, Lip Filler, Tear Trough).
- Starting price and neighborhood.
- Two buttons: "Book consult" (dark) and "View profile" (white with border).

**What the visitor does here:** Clicks one of the cards. Lands on the injector's full profile page (we will build that next).

**Important:** These are editorial picks, not paid. The card says so. This is the difference between us and our competitors.

---

## 7. From the Guides (Editorial)

**What it is:** A row of 3 treatment guide cards. Cream background to feel like a magazine.

**Why it's there:** This is the section that says "we are a publication, not a Yelp clone." It signals trust and depth.

**What's inside each card:**
- A photo at the top.
- Small label: "TREATMENT GUIDE".
- Title in serif (example: *Botox: The Complete Guide*).
- A 2-line summary.
- A small avatar + "Medically reviewed by Dr. [Name], MD".
- Read time and last updated date.

**What the visitor does here:** Clicks a card. Lands on a full treatment guide article.

**Why this matters:** Every guide shows a named medical reviewer with credentials. This is what Google rewards in medical content. It's also what makes patients trust us.

---

## 8. How We Verify

**What it is:** A 3-column section explaining our verification process.

**Why it's there:** Competitors don't have this section. We do. It explains exactly how we are different.

**What's inside each column:**
- A big green number (01, 02, 03).
- A short title.
- A 2-line explanation.

The three steps are:
1. **License check.** We verify every injector against the state medical board.
2. **Credential review.** Our medical advisory board reviews every profile before it goes live.
3. **Patient reviews moderated.** We check every review for authenticity. Injectors cannot delete reviews.

**Below the columns:** A link, "Read our editorial standards", for visitors who want the full policy.

**What the visitor does here:** Reads. Trusts us more. Maybe clicks the editorial standards link if they are very careful.

---

## 9. Real Patient Stories (Before and After)

**What it is:** 3 cards showing real patient before-and-after photos.

**Why it's there:** Patients want to see results before they book. This is the most-requested content on any aesthetic site.

**What's inside each card:**
- Two photos side by side (before on the left, after on the right).
- A treatment tag (Botox, Lip Filler, etc.) and "4 weeks post".
- "Treated by [injector name]".
- Neighborhood, city, and a tiny note: "Consent granted".

**What the visitor does here:** Looks at the photos. Optionally clicks to see the injector's profile.

**Important:** Every photo says "Consent granted". This protects us legally and shows we are not stealing photos.

**Below the cards:** A link, "View 6,200+ patient results", that goes to the full gallery.

---

## 10. FAQ (Common questions, plain answers)

**What it is:** Six expandable rows with the most common patient questions.

**Why it's there:** Two reasons. One, real patients have these questions. Two, Google rewards pages with FAQ schema markup. We get featured snippets.

**What's inside each row:**
- The question in bold (example: "How much does Botox cost?").
- A "+" icon on the right that rotates when clicked.
- The answer (60 to 100 words) appears when clicked.
- A small link at the end of each answer: "Read the full guide".

The six questions are:
1. How does injector.world verify providers?
2. Is Botox safe?
3. How much does Botox cost?
4. What's the difference between an MD, NP, and RN injector?
5. How do I prepare for my first appointment?
6. Are reviews on injector.world real?

**What the visitor does here:** Clicks the questions they care about. Gets a quick answer. If they want more, clicks through to the full guide.

---

## 11. Pre-Footer CTA (the navy section)

**What it is:** A dark navy section near the bottom with one more search bar.

**Why it's there:** Some visitors scroll all the way down without clicking anything. This is our second chance to get them searching.

**What's inside:**
- A small green label: "READY WHEN YOU ARE".
- A serif headline: *Find a verified injector in your city.*
- A search bar with a single field (city or ZIP) and a "Find injectors" button.

**What the visitor does here:** Types their city and searches. Goes to the city directory.

**Why this matters:** This is the second conversion moment. Without it, scrollers leave with no action taken.

---

## 12. Footer (the very bottom)

**What it is:** A big dark navy block at the bottom of every page.

**Why it's there:** Two reasons. One, it has the legal and policy links. Two, it has internal links that help SEO.

**What's inside:**
- Left column: logo, one-line description.
- Treatments column: 5 main treatments linked.
- Top Cities column: 5 main cities linked.
- Company column: About, Editorial standards, Medical advisory, Press, Careers.
- Legal column: Privacy, Terms, HIPAA notice, Contact.
- Very bottom line: "© 2026 injector.world. Information here is editorial and not medical advice."

**What the visitor does here:** Most don't click anything. The footer is for the few who need legal info, plus for Google to find more pages on our site.

---

## Quick recap (the whole page in one paragraph)

A visitor lands on the page. They see a serif headline and a search bar. They feel calm. They scroll. They see trust badges and big numbers. They scroll more. They see 12 treatments they recognize. Then 12 cities. Then 3 hand-picked injectors with credentials and ratings. Then 3 articles by a doctor. Then a 3-step explanation of how we verify everyone. Then real before-and-after photos. Then 6 common questions. Then one more search bar. Then a footer.

That's the journey. Trust, browse, search, leave with a click. Every section is a step in that flow.

---

## What you should test on a real person

Before you ship this to the client, show the homepage to two people who have never seen it.

Ask:
1. What do you think this site does? (Should be obvious in 5 seconds.)
2. Would you trust this site for a medical recommendation? (Should be yes.)
3. What would you click first? (Should be the search bar or a city.)

If they can answer all three quickly, the page is working. If not, tell me what they said and we fix it.
